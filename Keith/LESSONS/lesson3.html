In our study of Lesson 3.5, about the types of media we have dived into the ranging world of print and broadcast media understanding their formats and functions. Print media, which uses processes includes various forms like books, newspapers, magazines and brochures that often combine text and visuals. Typography plays a role in arranging written words while visual media uses pictures, images and graphics to communicate Graphic design is a process that helps organize and present information in mediums such as candy wrappers, billboards and T shirts. It serves purposes like providing information or persuasion or helping with organization and identification. Examples of media include graphics, cartoons and photography. Each playing a unique role in conveying information effectively. Moving on to broadcast media exploration we have looked into media, multimedia presentations and new forms of communication. Notable examples include radio broadcasts, television programs, podcasts augmented reality experiences, video games, blogs, and wikis. The concept of media convergence is also important as it signifies the blending of technologies on platforms, for organizing and distributing content.
